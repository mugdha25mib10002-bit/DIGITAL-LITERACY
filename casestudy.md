<h2>Case Study: Phishing Attack</h2>
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to fool people into sharing personal information like passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look real.

A phishing attack usually follows a simple process. First, the attacker sends a message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message creates urgency, asking the user to verify their account or warning about a problem. Then, the user is asked to click on a link, which takes them to a fake website that looks like the real one. When the user enters their details, the attacker collects this information and uses it for fraud.

For example, a college student received a message from what looked like their bank, asking them to update their account details urgently. Without checking, the student clicked the link and entered their login details and OTP. Soon after, money was deducted from their account.

Phishing can target students, working people, and anyone using online services. It can lead to loss of money, misuse of personal data, and account hacking. This shows the importance of being careful and checking information before taking any action online.
